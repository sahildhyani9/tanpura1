Intermediate Level

Mid-Level Course in Indian Classical Vocal Music (Hindustani Tradition)

What You‚Äôll Learn

Designed for singers who already possess a solid basic foundation in Hindustani classical vocal music, the Intermediate Level is where your artistry truly begins to blossom. This course deepens your command over melody, rhythm, and emotion, transforming familiar concepts into sophisticated, soulful expression‚Äîwhile keeping the joy of music-making alive at every step.

Core Learning Modules

  • Refining & Expanding Known Raags - Revisit the Raags you already know and unlock their hidden depths. Explore advanced Aaroh-Avaroh variations, intricate Pakad elaborations, Vistar (melodic expansion), and the subtle interplay of Vadi-Samvadi-Anuvadi notes. Learn to present a Raag with greater maturity through extended Alaap, Bol Alaap, and tasteful Meend (glides), giving each phrase its own emotional colour and personality.
  • Enhanced Taal & Sargam Mastery - Take your rhythm and note-pattern skills to new heights. Work with complex Taal cycles (e.g., Jhaptal, Rupak, Ektaal) and their advanced Thekas, Tihais, and Layakari (rhythmic divisions like 3:4, 5:4). Master faster and more intricate Palta and Alankaar sequences, including Sapat, Vakra, and Mishra patterns in multiple Layas‚Äîbuilding lightning precision, seamless voice control, and effortless breath management.
  • Introduction to Medium & Fast-Tempo Bandish - Move beyond slow compositions into Madhya Laya and Drut Bandish in various Raags. Learn the art of Taan (fast melodic passages)‚ÄîSapat Taan, Koot Taan, and Gamaka-heavy Taan‚Äîwhile maintaining perfect Sur and Laya. Develop the ability to weave Bol Bant (rhythmic word play) and graceful Murki into your singing, adding sparkle and individuality.
  • Personalised Pathways Based on Practice & Proficiency - Every student progresses at their own pace. If needed, the first few weeks/months are dedicated to confidently brushing up and solidifying earlier concepts before diving deeper. Your mentor designs a custom roadmap‚Äîwhether you wish to focus on Khayal, explore light-classical forms (Thumri/Dadra), or prepare for stage performance‚Äîensuring steady, stress-free growth.

Learning Approach & Support

  • One-on-One or Small-Group Attention ‚Äì Detailed voice correction, style guidance, and performance grooming
  • Regular Recording & Feedback ‚Äì Analyse your own Alaaps and Bandishes for continuous improvement
  • Curated Resources ‚Äì Access high-quality Bandish notations, Taal audio loops, and reference recordings of legendary artists
  • Performance Opportunities ‚Äì Periodic in-class recitals to build confidence and stage presence

Class Frequency & Commitment

Intermediate students enjoy complete flexibility: choose one or two sessions per week (60 minutes each), depending on your goals, practice routine, and comfort level. Most students find two sessions per week ideal for rapid and balanced progress, while dedicated learners often supplement with self-practice using provided material.
