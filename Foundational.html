Foundational Training

Beginner-Level Course in Indian Classical Vocal Music (Hindustani Tradition)

What You’ll Learn

Our beginner-level training is thoughtfully crafted to lay a robust and joyful musical foundation in the timeless art of Hindustani Classical Vocal Music. Whether you’re stepping into this world for the first time or seeking a structured restart, this course transforms curiosity into confidence—making every note, rhythm, and emotion accessible, engaging, and deeply rewarding.

Core Learning Modules

  • Understanding Notes (Swar / Swar Sthan)
    Dive into the soul of Indian music by mastering the seven primary swaras (Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha, Ni) and their precise placement (Swar Sthan) within the octave. Learn how each note breathes life into a Raag, its emotional character, and its unique position in melodic storytelling. Through guided listening and vocal exercises, you’ll develop an intuitive ear for pitch purity (Shruti Shuddhi) and begin recognizing microtones (Shruti) that define classical precision.
  • Introduction to Raag & Bandish
    Experience your first Raag in a gentle, immersive way. Explore its Aaroh (ascending), Avaroh (descending), Pakad (signature phrase), and Vadi-Samvadi (king-queen notes). Follow this with a simple Bandish—a short, lyrical composition in a chosen Raag and Taal—that serves as your melodic playground. Sing, feel, and internalize the Raag’s mood (Bhaav) through slow, expressive rendering (Vilambit Alaap) and playful variations, turning theory into heartfelt expression.
  • Basic Sargam Patterns (Alankaar / Palta)
    Build vocal agility and muscle memory with foundational Alankaar (systematic note patterns) and Palta (sequential exercises). These melodic drills strengthen breath control, voice modulation, and seamless transitions between notes. Practice in straight (Sapat) and wavy (Vakra) patterns across different speeds to enhance clarity, speed, and tonal accuracy—essential tools for lifelong vocal mastery.Understanding Swaras (Notes): Introduction to the seven basic notes (Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha, Ni) and their significance.
  • Elementary Rhythm & Taal Training
    Unlock the heartbeat of music with an introduction to Taal (rhythmic cycles). Get hands-on with Teentaal (16 beats) and Dadra (6 beats) using clap (taali) and wave (khali) patterns. Sync Sargam with Theka (basic tabla bols), recite rhythmic phrases, and explore Laya (tempo)—slow (Vilambit), medium (Madhya), and fast (Drut). This module fosters timing, coordination, and the joyful interplay between melody (Sur) and rhythm (Laya).Introduction to Ragas: Learning simple ragas like Yaman, Bhupali, and Kafi.

Learning Approach & Support

  • Interactive & Personalized: Small batch sizes (or 1:1 options) ensure individual attention, voice assessment, and tailored feedback.Understanding Notes (Swar / Swar Sthan)
  • Progressive Structure: Each concept builds on the previous, with weekly milestones and home practice guides.
  • Audio-Visual Aids: Access recorded Raag demonstrations, Taal loops, and notation sheets for reinforcement.
  • Fun Elements: Incorporate light Bhajans, folk-inspired Bandish, or creative improvisation to keep joy at the core.

Class Frequency & Commitment

To ensure steady progress and deep retention, students at the beginner level are encouraged to attend at least two sessions per week (45–60 minutes each). While three sessions per week are ideal for accelerated growth and muscle memory, a minimum of two is essential to maintain continuity, reinforce concepts, and cultivate daily practice habits.
