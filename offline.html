Offline (In-Studio) Class Policies & Guidelines

These guidelines are designed to create a disciplined, immersive, and joyful in-person learning environment while respecting the fixed nature of studio schedules.

Monthly Commitment & Class Structure

  • Offline classes operate on a fixed monthly batch system.
  • Each month consists of 8 or 12 classes, depending on whether you opt for 2 or 3 classes per week.
  • Your seat is reserved for the entire month, regardless of whether the month has 3, 4, or 5 weeks of classes.
  • Attendance options are pre-decided (e.g., 2 or 3 days per week) and remain consistent throughout the month.

Fee Payment

  • Fees are paid in full in advance by the 5th of every month.
  • No per-class fee adjustments are made for missed classes within the month.
  • The fee amount remains exactly the same whether the month has 3 weeks, 4 weeks, or 5 weeks of classes — ensuring complete transparency and fairness.

Attendance & Absence Rules

  • No rescheduling or make-up classes are provided for missed offline sessions — this applies to student absences as well as teacher holidays (except regular weekly offs on Saturday & Sunday).
  • If you know you will be absent (travel, exams, family events, etc.), kindly inform the teacher in advance out of courtesy and so your progress can be planned accordingly.
  • Regular attendance is essential for steady growth in the traditional guru-shishya parampara followed in our studio.

Fee Revision Policy

We deeply cherish long-term relationships with our students and have created a fee structure that rewards loyalty and commitment:
  • First year of learning: No fee increase.
  • Years 2, 3 & 4: A gentle, pre-announced increase once each year.
  • Years 5 & 6: Fees remain completely frozen – no increase for two full years.
  • Year 7 onwards: The cycle restarts (same pattern of gradual increases followed by freezes).
This policy ensures that dedicated students who continue their journey with us enjoy significant stability and savings over the years.

General Studio Etiquette

  • Arrive 5–10 minutes early with your notebook, tanpura (or app), and a calm mind.
  • Inform the teacher in advance if you will be late or absent.
  • Maintain a quiet and respectful atmosphere in the studio — phones on silent.
  • Wear comfortable attire suitable for prolonged sitting and voice practice.
We’re delighted to welcome you into our physical gurukul space where the magic of live, in-person riyaz truly comes alive.
